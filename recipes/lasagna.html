Lasagna

Description

My grandmother was a bechemel farmer on the shores of the Azerbaijanian sea (not the one in South Dakota) and one day as she watched the sun set in the East (or was it West) she realised that she could combine some of the world's most delicious ingredients to make a dish which honestly just takes way too long to make when it basically just kind of tastes like spaghetti which you could knock out in like 15 minutes if you were sufficiently motivated. But whatever, here's my recipe for Lasagna (pronounced La-Sag-Na).

Ingredients

  • Store bought Lasagna Sheets, nobody has time to make them by hand on top of the rest of this
  • A jar of Pasta Sauce, probably a super big one because this takes a lot
  • Bechemal(apparently), but i think just using like a kilogram of tasty cheese and mozzarella is a far better topping
  • Some mince, probably beef, just get like a normal amount but then maybe a little bit more than that.

Method

  1. Cook the mince until it loses all it's moisture because you don't understand heat control or not overcrowding the pan
  2. When it's brown-adjacent(like grey-ish because your cooking skills would get you deported from Italy) pour in the pasta sauce and heat it up til it's hot (duh)
  3. Layer it in a deep pan, alternating with the lasagna sheets (we've all seen this done before I'm not going into specifics
  4. Cover it in the aforementioned metric ton of cheese
  5. Bake it until it looks like it won't vibe with your guts and the cheese is delciously melty and burnt because you forgot it and the children are screaming for McDonalds
  6. Eat far too much and consider bulimia before falling into a food coma for 2-7 days
  7. Serves like 4 people, maybe 6, all depends on you
